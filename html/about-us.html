Our Team

As a team we are made up of future engineers, scientists, programmers, and various other majors working together to achieve our group goal: Make a completely autonomous SailBot. We meet every week to discuss plans and prepare the boat, though most of the hard work is done individually. As a group, we enjoy sailing at the local Jamaica Pond, as well as the Charles River.

Our History

Our club was originally started by two Senior Mechanical Engineers (Charles O’Connor/Sean Suri) as their Capstone project. They soon fell in love with the group dynamic and gathered a group of interested Engineers. SailBot became an official recognized Club Fall 2013 with Justin Hynes-Bruell as President and Dan Thompson as Vice President. We initially rallied a massive group of around 50 interested students, though after experiencing the micromanagement involved, downsized the club to around 10 Solid members.

Joseph Lagalla will be leading the club forward until Spring 2015.

Our Values

Here at NU Sailbot, we share Northeastern's values:

  • Contribution - We aim to contribute to society by making a relatively efficient vessel that can navigate on its own. We can see real world applications of this such as conducting unmanned investigations out at sea..
  • Diversity - Our team consists of different cultures and majors, allowing for a broader scope in our endeavors. We have people with majors varying from Computer and Mechanical Engineering, to Chemistry and Computer Science.
  • Engagement - Thanks to Northeastern’s commitment to engagement, our team has more real world experience than most colleges granted to us by our Co-Op program. Many of our members bring with them real world experience from their prior experiences.
  • Integrity - As with any good organization, integrity is also a key factor. We value honesty between our members, our supporters, and in competition.
  • Opportunity - As a Northeastern team, we pride ourselves on offering a variety of opportunities to our members, whether it be teaching them an entirely new programming language or giving them the chance to go sailing at a local pond. We like to think that we provide our members with a road range of new experiences and learning.
